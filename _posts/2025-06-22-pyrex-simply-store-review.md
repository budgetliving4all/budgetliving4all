## What you get

The 18-piece set includes nine glass containers with nine lids. Sizes run from 1-cup round up to 7-cup round and 6-cup rectangular, which covers everything from a single portion of rice to a full batch of soup. The lids are color-coded by size, which helps when you are digging through a cabinet.

All the glass is tempered, meaning it can go from the freezer directly to a microwave or oven without cracking from the temperature change. The lids are BPA-free plastic. Glass pieces are oven-safe up to 450°F with lids removed. The lids themselves are microwave and top-rack dishwasher safe.

The set comes with a 2-year limited warranty on both glass and lids.


## What works well

**The glass itself is genuinely durable.** Multiple independent reviewers, including The Kitchn, have used these for years without cracking or clouding. Pyrex glass does not absorb odors, stains, or flavors from food — after dozens of uses with garlic, tomato sauce, and strongly seasoned proteins, it still cleans up easily.

**The size range is useful.** Most meal prep containers come in one size. The variety here means you can use a 1-cup container for a portion of hummus or a sauce, a 2-cup for a side vegetable, and a 4-cup for a full protein and grain portion. That flexibility matters when you are portioning a full week of food.

**Stackable in the fridge.** The flat lids stack securely, which matters when you have five or six containers taking up shelf space.

**You can see what is inside without opening it.** A small thing that turns out to be useful every day.


## What does not work as well

**The lids are not liquid-tight.** They fit snugly and will keep food fresh in the fridge, but if you tip a container sideways, liquid will leak. Fine for solid meal prep portions, not suitable for soups or saucy dishes you are putting in a bag.

**The lids crack faster than you would expect.** This is the most common complaint across Amazon, Pyrex's own site, and Home Depot reviews. Running the lids through the dishwasher repeatedly accelerates this. Most users who have had the lids crack early were dishwashing them regularly. Hand washing extends their life considerably.

**The glass is heavier than plastic.** Five containers of food in a fridge is not an issue. Carrying them to work every day is something to think about if weight matters to you.

**Sizing runs smaller than some people expect.** The 2-cup containers hold one reasonable portion of a single component — a grain or a vegetable — not a full mixed meal. For a full portioned lunch, the 4-cup containers are the ones you will use most.


## How it compares

At $30-35 for 18 pieces, the Pyrex Simply Store is priced competitively against other glass sets. Cheaper plastic alternatives exist, but glass does not warp in the microwave, does not stain from tomato-based foods, and does not need replacing as frequently. Over two or three years, glass typically costs less per use than budget plastic.

If liquid-tight lids matter to you, the Pyrex Freshlock line uses a four-locking-tab lid that seals properly. It costs more per container, but solves the leaking issue. That set is worth considering if you are regularly transporting soups or dishes with a lot of sauce.


## Who it makes sense for

If you are meal prepping solid foods — proteins, grains, roasted vegetables — and storing them in the fridge through the week, this set does the job well. The glass holds up, the sizes cover most use cases, and the price is reasonable.

If you need to transport liquid-heavy meals, or if you are hard on kitchen equipment and want the lids to survive the dishwasher long-term, look at the Pyrex Freshlock set or a container with locking tabs.
