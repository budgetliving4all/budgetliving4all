🌡️
Food safety note {% if food_type == "chicken" or food_type == "poultry" or food_type == "turkey" %}

According to FoodSafety.gov, all poultry must reach an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C) before eating. Use a food thermometer inserted into the thickest part of the meat, away from bone.

{% elsif food_type == "ground-beef" or food_type == "ground beef" %}

According to FoodSafety.gov, ground beef must reach an internal temperature of 160°F (71°C). Color alone is not a reliable indicator — always use a thermometer.

{% elsif food_type == "beef" or food_type == "beef-steak" %}

According to FoodSafety.gov, beef steaks and roasts should reach a minimum internal temperature of 145°F (63°C), then rest for at least 3 minutes before eating.

{% elsif food_type == "pork" %}

According to FoodSafety.gov, pork chops and roasts should reach 145°F (63°C) and rest for at least 3 minutes before eating.

{% elsif food_type == "ground-pork" or food_type == "ground pork" %}

According to FoodSafety.gov, ground pork must reach an internal temperature of 160°F (71°C) before eating.

{% elsif food_type == "fish" or food_type == "seafood" %}

According to FoodSafety.gov, fish should reach an internal temperature of 145°F (63°C), or cook until the flesh is opaque and separates easily with a fork.

{% elsif food_type == "eggs" %}

According to FoodSafety.gov, cook eggs until both the yolk and white are firm. Egg dishes such as frittatas should reach 160°F (71°C).

{% elsif food_type == "leftovers" or food_type == "reheating" %}

According to FoodSafety.gov, all leftovers should be reheated to an internal temperature of 165°F (74°C) before eating, regardless of the original food type.

{% else %}

According to FoodSafety.gov, always use a food thermometer to confirm food has reached a safe internal temperature. Never rely on color or texture alone.

{% endif %}

Cooked meat and poultry keeps 3 to 4 days refrigerated at 40°F or below. Full storage chart at FoodSafety.gov.
